Milk and Honey

In the scriptures (The Holy Bible and The Book of Mormon), these words end up popping up together. Why do you think that is? I like to think that it is because in persian, which may have contributed to Hebrew, the word for milk/honey is a homograph (it's like English's their/there/ and they're.) Thus, translators would have a hard time determining which was which. Indeed, ancient speakers might have even though of them as being very similar: they are both produced by a natural process, are sweet, and are indicators of life.
